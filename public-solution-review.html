Our verdict

The most intuitive CRM we tested. monday.com turns your sales pipeline into a colourful, drag-and-drop board that small teams actually enjoy using — backed by strong automations and a genuinely usable free plan. Power users may want deeper reporting, but for most SA SMEs it's the best all-rounder.

Introduction

monday.com started life as a work-management tool, and that DNA is exactly why its CRM feels different. Instead of rigid forms, your leads and deals live on visual boards you can shape around how your business actually sells — making it one of the gentlest CRMs to adopt for a team that has never used one before.

For this review we ran monday.com CRM through a typical South African small-business workflow: capturing inbound leads, moving deals through a pipeline, automating follow-ups, and reporting on what closed. Below we break down the features, pricing in rand, and the honest pros and cons so you can decide whether it fits your team.

Main Features

Visual, customisable pipelines

The headline feature is the drag-and-drop board. Each deal is a row you can colour-code, group, and move between stages in a click. You can build multiple pipelines for different products or regions without touching a settings menu.

Automations without code

monday.com's recipe-style automations let you trigger emails, assign owners, and create tasks when a deal changes stage — no developer required. It's one of the more approachable automation builders in this category.

Email & activity tracking

Connect your inbox to log emails against contacts, schedule follow-ups, and see a full activity timeline for every deal. Notifications keep the whole team aligned without endless status meetings.

Dashboards & reporting

Drag widgets onto a dashboard to track pipeline value, win rate, and team activity in real time. Reporting is strong for day-to-day visibility, though analytics-heavy teams may eventually want more depth.
