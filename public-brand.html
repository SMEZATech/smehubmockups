Introduction

Yoco is a mobile card-payment platform built specifically for South African small businesses. Founded in 2015, it set out to remove the barriers that kept traders, restaurants and salons from accepting cards — high monthly rentals, lengthy bank approvals, and complex contracts. Instead, Yoco offers affordable card machines on a pay-per-use model, so you only pay when you make a sale.

For this profile we looked at how Yoco fits a typical SA small-business workflow: buying a once-off machine, taking card and tap payments, tracking sales in the app, and accessing working capital. Below we break down the features, pricing in rand, and the honest pros and cons so you can decide whether it fits your business.

Main Features

Card machines — Go, Neo, Khumo & Khumo Print

Yoco offers a range of card machines for every counter. The Go is the entry-level reader that pairs with your phone, the Neo is a standalone all-in-one, while Khumo and Khumo Print add a larger screen, built-in connectivity and an on-device receipt printer for busier shops.

Yoco Capital — cash advance

Once you have a trading history with Yoco, you can apply for Yoco Capital, a cash advance repaid automatically as a small percentage of your daily card sales — useful working capital without a traditional loan application.

Yoco app — sales & insights

The free Yoco app turns every sale into data: track turnover, see your best-selling items, manage refunds, and view settlement reports from your phone. It doubles as a lightweight point-of-sale for small teams.

Yoco Link — payment links

Send a secure payment link by WhatsApp, email or social media and get paid remotely — handy for deposits, invoices and online orders without a full e-commerce store.

Gateway & integrations

Yoco's online gateway and integrations let you accept payments on your website or connect to point-of-sale and accounting tools, so card, online and in-person sales all flow into one place.
