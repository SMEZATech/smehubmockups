Buyer's Guide

Why you need a CRM Core Features How Pricing Works Compare Platforms Get a Quote FAQs

Relying on spreadsheets and memory to manage your sales pipeline is a guaranteed way to lose revenue. A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system acts as the single source of truth for your business.

It records every email, logs every call, and automates your follow-ups. Whether you are a solo consultant in Sandton or a 20-person logistics team in Cape Town, choosing the right platform depends heavily on your specific operational bottlenecks and team size.

Core Features

Pipeline Visibility

Visualise your sales process. Move deals through stages (e.g., Lead, Proposal, Won) to identify bottlenecks.

Workflow Automation

Set up triggers to automatically send welcome emails or schedule follow-up tasks, saving hours of manual work.

Local Integrations

Ensure the CRM connects with the tools your SA business already uses—like Xero or Sage for accounting, local VoIP providers, or the WhatsApp Business API for customer communication.

How Pricing Works

Per-User Subscription – Cloud-based CRM software typically charges per user, per month. For South African businesses, this can range from a robust R0 starter tier up to R800+ per user for enterprise features. Most providers offer a 14-day or 30-day trial.

The Forex Trap – Be mindful of currency fluctuations. If a US-based CRM charges $25/user/month, a weak Rand can dramatically inflate your software costs overnight. This is why platforms billing locally in ZAR (like Zoho) are highly attractive.

Hidden Costs – Watch out for add-ons. You may have to pay separately for marketing automation hubs, advanced AI tools, or increased API limits as your business grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of a CRM in SA?

Cloud-based CRM software typically ranges from R150 to R800 per user per month for small businesses in South Africa, depending on the features and integrations required. Many offer no-cost starter tiers for startups.

Do I need a CRM if I just use Excel?

Excel is great for flat data storage, but a CRM automates your workflows. It actively reminds you to follow up, tracks email opens, logs call notes, and ensures no valuable leads are forgotten when your sales team gets busy.
