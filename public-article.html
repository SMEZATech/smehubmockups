Starting Up

Kasi Money Starts With How You Earn

KK
Karabo Kgophane

20 Jan 2026 · 5 min read

Wealth in the township economy doesn't start with how you spend — it starts with how you earn. Here's how SA founders are rethinking income from the ground up.

For too long, financial advice aimed at small business owners has focused on cutting costs. But for founders building in the kasi economy, the bigger lever is income design: how predictable, diversified and scalable your earning is.

Build more than one income stream

Relying on a single product or client is the fastest way to a cash-flow crisis. The most resilient SMEs layer complementary streams — a core product, a service add-on, and a recurring element that smooths the lean months.

  • Core offering: the thing customers already pay you for
  • Service layer: installation, delivery, or support
  • Recurring layer: subscriptions, maintenance, or memberships

Price for value, not just to compete

Undercutting the shop next door feels safe, but it caps your growth. Price against the value and convenience you deliver, and communicate it clearly. Customers in every market pay more for trust and reliability.

"Don't compete on price you can't sustain — compete on the value only you provide."

Reinvest before you spend

When the money comes in, the temptation is to spend. The founders who scale treat a fixed share of every rand as reinvestment — into stock, tools, or skills that raise tomorrow's earning power.
