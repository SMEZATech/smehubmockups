How we earn money

SME South Africa is a free resource for entrepreneurs, and producing it costs money. To keep the platform open and growing, we earn revenue from a few sources: advertising and sponsorships from partner brands, affiliate commissions when readers sign up for or buy products we link to, paid brand listings in our directories, and sponsored content created with or for partners.

This means that some of the deals, tools, funders and service providers featured on this site are commercial relationships. When you click certain links, fill in a funding enquiry, or purchase a product through us, we may be paid a referral fee or commission — at no extra cost to you.

Editorial independence

Commercial relationships never determine our editorial opinions, review scores, rankings or recommendations. Our writers and reviewers assess products and services on their merits, and a partner cannot buy a better score, a higher ranking, or favourable coverage.

Advertising and sponsorship are kept structurally separate from our editorial team. If a paid partner also appears in an independent review or guide, that placement is earned on merit and labelled accordingly.

How we label sponsored & affiliate content

We believe you should always be able to tell when something is paid for. We label commercial content clearly and consistently:

  • Sponsored — content paid for by a brand carries a visible "Sponsored" or "Paid partnership" tag.
  • Advertisement — display ad slots and promoted listings are marked as advertising.
  • Affiliate — pages and links that may earn us a commission carry an affiliate notice near the relevant links.
  • Brand listing — paid directory and funding listings are flagged as featured or partner placements.

Affiliate links & commissions

Some outbound links on this site are affiliate links. If you click one and go on to sign up, apply or buy, the partner may pay us a commission. You never pay more because of an affiliate link, and using one is entirely optional — you can always go directly to a provider instead.

We only join affiliate programmes for products and services we consider genuinely relevant to South African small businesses, and an affiliate relationship does not guarantee a recommendation.

Disclosure standards (FTC / ASA)

Our disclosures are designed to meet recognised advertising standards, including the principles of the U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC) endorsement guidelines and the South African Advertising Regulatory Board (ARB) and ASA codes. In short: material commercial connections are disclosed clearly, prominently and close to the relevant content, in plain language — not buried in fine print.

Questions & contact

If you have any questions about this disclosure, how we earn money, or how a particular piece of content is labelled, we are happy to explain. Contact us at hello@smesouthafrica.co.za or via our contact page. For advertising and partnership enquiries, see our Advertise page.
