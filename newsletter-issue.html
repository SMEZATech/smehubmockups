The R0 marketing stack that actually converts

By Karabo Kgophane

5 min read·12 Jun 2026

Hello Founders,

The biggest myth in small-business marketing is that you need money to start. You don't. What you need is a tight system: a clear message, two or three channels you actually maintain, and a way to capture the people who show interest before they forget you exist.

This week we're breaking down the exact zero-rand stack we see working for SA founders right now — the tools, the order to set them up in, and the channels that quietly waste your time.

Start with the message, not the tool

Most founders jump straight to "which app should I use?" That's backwards. Before you touch a single tool, you need one sentence that explains who you help and what changes for them. If you can't say it plainly, no amount of automation will save the funnel.

Write it down. Test it on five customers. Only then start wiring up channels.

"A free tool you actually use beats a paid one you set up once and forget. Consistency is the whole strategy."

The free stack, in order

Here's the sequence we'd build today, every piece on a free tier:

  • One owned channel — an email list. Social can disappear overnight; your list can't. Kit's free tier covers your first 10 000 subscribers.
  • One content channel — pick the single platform where your buyers already spend time, and post there twice a week. Not five platforms once a month.
  • One capture point — a simple landing page with a single call to action, so interest turns into an email address.
  • One feedback loop — a recurring way to ask customers what nearly stopped them from buying.

The channels quietly wasting your time

Be honest about reach. Posting daily to an audience of twelve, chasing every new app, and redesigning your logo for the third time are all comfortable ways to feel busy without moving revenue. Cut them and put the hours into the four pieces above.

If a channel hasn't produced a single conversation with a real buyer in 30 days, it isn't a channel — it's a hobby. Park it, write down what you'd need to see to revisit it, and move on without guilt.

Wire the capture before the traffic

The most common own-goal we see: founders pour effort into reach, send people to a homepage with no clear next step, and watch the interest evaporate. Build the capture point first. A single landing page with one promise and one email field will out-earn a beautiful site with five competing buttons.

Run it every single week

None of this works as a one-off. The founders who win treat the stack as a weekly rhythm: one message, posted to one channel, capturing to one list, with one question asked of customers. Small, boring, repeated. That repetition is the entire competitive advantage.

Build the system, keep it small, and run it every single week. That's the whole game.

— Karabo & the SME South Africa team
